This is a **Block Definition Diagram (BDD)** that illustrates the **conceptual architecture and structure** of a Water Distribution System.

At the center of the diagram is the **Water Distribution System Boundary** block, which serves as the focal point for several external and internal relationships.

### **External Entities and Interfaces**
The system interacts with several external "blocks," representing entities outside its immediate internal structure:
*   **Electrical Utility:** Connected as an electrical utility interface.
*   **Contaminantion Dispose:** A block designated for the disposal of contamination (noted with a typo as "Contaminantion" in the source).
*   **Operator:** Represents the human or system entity that operates the water distribution system.
*   **Supply Reservoir:** The source or storage point that provides the water supply.
*   **Water Tower:** Another storage or pressure-regulation entity associated with the system.

### **Internal System Concepts**
The diagram also outlines the key conceptual subsystems that make up the internal structure of the water distribution system:
*   **Pump Subsystem Concept:** Represents the mechanical components responsible for moving water.
*   **Pipe Subsystem Concept:** The network of infrastructure for water transport.
*   **Monitor and Control Subsystem Concept:** The system responsible for tracking performance and managing operations.
*   **Water Sanitation Concept:** The processes or components focused on ensuring water quality.
